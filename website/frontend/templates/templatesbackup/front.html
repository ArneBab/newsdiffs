Tracking Online News Articles Over Time

NewsDiffs allows you to compare evolving versions of online news articles as they change over time, starting with The New York Times and CNN.

Sometimes the changes are minor â€” small edits in language or correction of spelling mistakes. Other times, the stories change and evolve rapidly, as a result of breaking news. Occasionally, the lede and substance of an article changes, as in the example to the right.

The project is inspired by the version control tracking used in computer programming.

NewsDiff tracking began on June 17, 2012, as the project was inspired by the Knight Mozilla MIT Hackathon.

You can browse our repository of articles.

Or you can take a look at some of the examples of articles that have changed.
